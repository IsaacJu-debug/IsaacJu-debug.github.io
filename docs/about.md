I'm a **PhD Candidate in Energy Resources Engineering at Stanford University** and **Co-Founder of [Earthflow AI](https://earthflow.ai)**. I work at the intersection of **Graph Neural Networks**, **Diffusion Models**, and **AI-driven simulations** for subsurface flow dynamics and carbon capture.

## Research Focus

My research centers on developing scalable machine learning algorithms for high-fidelity simulations on polyhedral meshes. I'm particularly interested in:

- **Graph Neural Networks** for physics-based simulations
- **High-Performance Computing (HPC)** with OpenMP, MPI, CUDA, and VTK
- **Deep learning surrogate models** for accelerating scientific computing workflows
- **AI for energy and environmental applications**

## Experience Highlights

At **Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory**, my deep-learning-based surrogate models achieved a **100x speedup** in data assimilation workflows compared to traditional physics-based simulators. I'm also an active contributor to **[GEOS](https://www.geosx.org/)**, an open-source exascale HPC platform for subsurface simulations.

Previously at **TotalEnergies E&P USA**, I developed advanced deep-learning models using PyTorch-Geometric to simulate subsurface flow dynamics. At **NexaAI**, I led the R&D team deploying deep generative models for practical applications.

## Current Work

I'm building **[Earthflow AI](https://earthflow.ai)** to bring cutting-edge AI to earth science and energy applications. My research focuses on **Adaptive Physics Transformers** and **Function-Space Diffusion Models** for subsurface simulations, pushing the boundaries of AI for accelerating scientific computing.
